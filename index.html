Welcome

Welcome to the Digital Home of Dr. Mike Teng

Is downsizing the only answer to solving corporate woes? Why is your company struggling to grow? Why do businesses fall sick, recover, and subsequently succumb to the exact same ailments? If these questions sound familiar, chances are your company is in a slump, and it may feel like the situation will not improve anytime soon.

Like many enterprises, yours may have fallen ill due to "corporate viruses"—such as global economic slowdowns, the rapid disruptions of globalization, unforeseen crises, or incompetent management. However, there is hope. Even dying companies can attain holistic, long-term recovery by applying a new, breakthrough prescription of surgery, resuscitation, and nursing.

The Blueprint for Recovery: A Best-Selling Book, Not Just a Guide

Using practical medical analogies, Corporate Turnaround: Nursing a Sick Company Back to Health is not merely a guide; it is a foundational, best-selling book that prescribes effective, easy-to-administer turnaround techniques in precisely the right doses.

Reformulated to be universally applicable and user-friendly, this book dismantles the misconception that an expensive, adversarial bankruptcy is the only way out of crippling debt. Instead, it walks distressed business owners through practical, out-of-court alternatives. This three-step methodology has earned the praise of world-renowned business tycoons and academia, proudly carrying endorsements from international management guru Professor Philip Kotler, alongside prominent business tycoons Mr. Oei Hong Leong and Dr. Y.Y. Wong.

Holistic Restoration: Secular and Spiritual

The true essence of Dr. Teng's mission extends far beyond balance sheets, debt haircuts, and creditor negotiations. Business crises do not happen in a vacuum; they take a devastating emotional, physical, and spiritual toll on founders, their employees, and their families.

These more than twenty five books authored by Dr Teng recognize that a company is not an inanimate object, but a living organism. The intellectual, physical, and spiritual aspects of a business leader's life are intricately intertwined. When a company is "sick," its leaders are almost always operating from a place of intense fear, isolation, and spiritual exhaustion.

Therefore, a core purpose of Dr. Teng's writing is to serve as a bridge to both secular and spiritual restoration. It is an open invitation for burdened SME owners to seek Jesus Christ for the spiritual help, grace, and ultimate wisdom that secular business strategies simply cannot provide. By integrating practical corporate turnaround mechanics with profound spiritual insights, this platform offers a holistic path to wellness, reminding leaders that while their earthly businesses can be restructured and saved, their ultimate security rests in faith.
